The 'toggle' effect

toggle alternates between the show() and hide() methods.

Click the link to show the hidden div. Then click it again to hide it.

<a id="target" href="#"> Click Here </a>
<p id="statement"> This is a sentence </p>
<div id="box" style="width:500px; height:100px; border:1px solid #000; background-color:yellow; display:none;">
  This is a rectangle
  </div>

$('#target').on("click", function() {
  //code to be executed when the button is clicked
  $('p#statement').hide();
  $('div#box').toggle();
});

Simple example

<button id="btn1">Toggle between hiding and showing the paragraphs</button>

<p id="p1">This is a paragraph with little content.</p>

$("button#btn1").click(function(){
  $("p#p1").toggle();

Adding a time parameter

The optional speed parameter can take the following values: "slow", "fast", or milliseconds.

<button id="btn2">Toggle between hiding and showing the paragraphs</button>

  <p id="p2">This is a paragraph with little content.</p>

  $("button#btn1").click(function(){
    $("p#p2").toggle(1000);
  });
